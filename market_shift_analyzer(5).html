3 Power Moves

After identifying a favored sector via institutional flow, apply these three technical filters to isolate the highest-probability security.

01
Heartbeat Pattern
The price chart shows consistent higher highs and higher lows — a visual rhythm of persistent buying pressure indicating institutional accumulation has resolved fundamental uncertainty.
↑ HH + HL sequence ↓ LH + LL pattern
Look for: sequential advances, pullbacks holding above prior support, increasing consolidation at higher levels, volume confirmation on advance phases.
02
50-Day Moving Average
The 50-DMA is the "line in the sand" for trend assessment. Price above an upward-sloping 50-DMA = institutional support. Breaks below = weakening conviction.
↑ Price > 50-DMA, slope up ↓ Break below 50-DMA
Tests of the 50-DMA that hold with high volume are strongest signals of institutional support. Use as risk management trigger — not in isolation.
03
Volume = Power
Institutional size creates measurable trading activity. High volume on up days = accumulation. Low volume on down days = lack of distribution. Volume expansion at key levels = conviction.
↑ High vol on advances ↓ High vol on declines
Price moves on heavy volume carry far more weight than those on light trading. Volume contraction = lack of institutional commitment.

Rule 1 — Profit-Taking & Exit Strategy (Sell Schedule)

A disciplined sell schedule to prevent round-tripping gains — letting a winning position run all the way back to zero. Locks in profits progressively while leaving a runner.

Rule 2 — Risk Management (2% Portfolio / 20% Stop-Loss)

Professional traders pair the profit-taking rule with strict loss-mitigation benchmarks. Two hard limits protect capital at both position and portfolio level.

2%
Portfolio Rule — Max Account Loss Per Stock
Never allow a single stock position to lose more than 2% of your total account value. Protects overall capital from a single catastrophic failure. On a $25,370 portfolio → max loss per name = ~$507.
Your portfolio: ~$25,370
Max loss/position: $507
20%
Individual Stop-Loss — Max Loss Per Position
Use a maximum 20% stop-loss on any individual position. Liberal enough to avoid getting whipsawed by normal volatility, but ensures you cut and run before a 20% loss becomes 50% or 80%. No exceptions.
Logic: 20% down → need 25% to recover. 50% down → need 100%.
Cut early. Reload at better prices.
Rule 3 — Diversification Benchmark (20–30–40 Holdings)

How many stocks should you actually own? Research shows 90% of diversification benefits are achieved with just 12–18 stocks. Here's the spectrum:

20
The Target
Sufficient diversification. Most individual investors can effectively manage 5–10 stocks. 20 is the ceiling for active management without becoming a closet index fund.
30
The Warning Zone
At 30 holdings you start losing your edge. Marginal diversification benefit drops sharply. Each new name adds complexity but minimal risk reduction.
40
The Limit — Closet Index
>40 stocks = you're performing like the S&P 500 but with more work, more fees, and more taxes. You've lost your selection alpha entirely.
